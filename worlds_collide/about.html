How do we identify ourselves? Is a person separate from their culture or a product of it? In Haruki Murakami's novels, there are often two forces driving the plot: the Self and the Other. Murakami's characters go through an identity crisis, fighting a culturally or ethnically imposed identity, in search of their own. The Self can be viewed as the quest to define or understand your own personal identity in the midst of the opposing 'Other' forces of society and culture.
